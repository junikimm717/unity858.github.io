TeX

Refer to my GH repo. Comprehensive list of that repo's contents:
  • LaTeX style files: seto.cls, what most of the papers on this webpage are typesetted using;
    universal.sty, usable with other people's styles, contains various macros; tropical.cls, a recoloring of Warsaw Beamer but I've added my own things to it as well, including slide numbering in the form (current / total);
  • Simple .py scripts, some of them are NT-themed procedures, the others are from the dark, boring days of 9th grade Honors Precalculus. I would've done it into AP Calculus too, but as I never figured out how to use Python to manipulate with abstract expressions other than polynomials, it's far more limited in scope than the precalc counterpart.
