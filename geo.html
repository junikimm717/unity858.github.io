Some geometers' apology

As the title suggests, I/we "propose to put forward an apology" in emulation of Hardy's essay of similar title -- this is why we still do geometry.

Artistics

Yes, this may be a thousands-years-old subject, often dealt with today using very recent computer software or algebraic methods, but it can never lose its aesthetic taste. By default when people say `art' they mean painting and/or drawing. Much less people would think of the art of essays. Geometry is to math what painting and drawing are to art.

Uselessness

In the past, it was geometry that was used every turn: aiming cannons, constructing structures like temples or forums. The other three subjects were relegated to recreation in the form of 'numbers puzzles'.

Today, the tables have flipped: algebra is used in ,combinatorics is used in CS, and number theory is used in cryptography. By contrast, geometry has become an art and an art only. For, nobody judges a Picasso painting by its applications to business (apart from its monetary value?).

Ending remarks

I too find writing about math a "melancholic experience"-- perhaps the time spent writing this would be better spent doing geometry itself...
